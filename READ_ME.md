Industrial Problem Statement
Traditional laboratory testing protocols create production delays. Waiting for analysis results leads to manufacturing bottlenecks. If a production batch drops below quality standards during processing, operators often notice the defect after packaging. This latency causes significant material waste and financial loss.

Statistical Verification
Ordinary Least Squares regression analysis establishes the chemical drivers of product quality. The validation confirms volatile acidity and alcohol content determine final scores.

R-Squared: 0.3242

Acidity P-Value: 3.62E-40

Alcohol P-Value: 1.80E-67

The low p-values eliminate the possibility of random correlation. Management receives mathematical certainty for operational adjustments.

Data Pipeline Architecture
Ingestion: The automated script reads raw data and removes duplicate records.

Feature Engineering: The system calculates active preservative stability ratios.

Modeling: A Random Forest classifier evaluates batches instantly.

Storage: The pipeline writes the analytical predictions into a SQL database.

Operational Financial Impact
Early intervention during fermentation costs 1,500 dollars. Reactive waste removal after packaging costs 13,000 dollars. The automated pipeline saves 11,500 dollars per deflected incident.
